Regional health overview for 5 Jacksonville-area counties

Key Insight

Duval County has the highest health burden (score: 50.4) with 23 HPSA shortage and 34.7% chronic disease prevalence — nearly 2x the need of Nassau County (score: 27.9).

Nassau and St. Johns counties have the lowest healthcare needs, making them ideal benchmarks for wellness program effectiveness.

What These Scores Mean (0-100 scale):

  • Primary Care Access (30pts): HRSA HPSA shortage score — higher = harder to find doctors
  • Chronic Disease (30pts): CDC PLACES prevalence — % with diabetes, obesity, or asthma
  • Air Quality (15pts): EPA unhealthy AQI days (2023 data)
  • Hazard Risk (15pts): FEMA Natural Disaster Risk Index
  • Respiratory Activity (10pts): CDC state-level flu/COVID/RSV — currently Minimal
