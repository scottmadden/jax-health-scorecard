Key Insights

▸ Duval County concentration: All top 10 highest-need schools are in Duval, driven by high chronic disease rates (20-32%) and doctor shortages.
▸ Dual burden matters: 21 schools face BOTH high chronic disease AND doctor shortages — these need immediate nurse placement.
▸ Nassau outperforms: All Nassau schools score under 20 (healthiest region). Use as benchmark for wellness programs.
▸ Within-county variance: Chronic disease varies 13 percentage points within Duval. School-level data reveals what county averages hide.
▸ Nurse coverage gap: 12 schools (9%) lack nurses, including 1 high-need school(s). Estimated cost to fill all gaps: $2,760,000.

Understanding Health Grades

A
Excellent
Minimal health needs. Strong community health and adequate medical support.
B
Good
Low health needs. Standard wellness programs are sufficient.
C
Fair
Moderate needs. Enhanced health services and monitoring recommended.
D
Needs Action
High needs. Urgent placement of full-time nurse and wellness programs required.
F
Critical
Critical needs. Immediate intervention required. Multiple health support gaps.

Grades reflect combined health burden (chronic disease, doctor availability, environmental factors) and available support (nurse staffing).
